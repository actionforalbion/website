Timeline of events

The last 6 years have been tumultuous for the club, with 11 managers, 4 CEOs and an array of financial mismanagement.

2016

  1. July 2016

    Gouchuan Lai becomes controlling shareholder of WBA

    He takes a 88% stake and inherits a £3.7m loan previously borrowed by ex-chairman Jeremy Peace.

  2. August 2016

    John Williams appointed as Chair

  3. December 2016

    Mark Jenkins (CEO) leaves WBA

    He is replaced by Martin Goodman, former Stoke City Finance Director.

2017-2020

  1. November 2017

    Tony Pulis leaves as Head Coach

    He is replaced by Alan Pardew

  2. February 2018

    John Williams (Chair) and Martin Goodman (CEO) both leave WBA

    Mark Jenkins reappointed as CEO.

  3. April 2018

    Alan Pardew leaves as Head Coach

    He is replaced by Darren Moore.

  4. May 2018

    WBA are relegated from the Premier League

    Despite their heavier spending in comparison to prior seasons. Li Piyue appointed as Chair.

  5. March 2019

    Darren Moore leaves as Head Coach

    He is replaced by James Shan (interim).

  6. June 2019

    Slaven Bilić appointed as Head Coach

    He is replaced by Darren Moore.

2020-2022

  1. August 2020

    Mark Jenkins (CEO) leaves WBA for a second time

    He is replaced by existing Director Xu Ke.

  2. December 2020

    Slaven Bilić leaves as Head Coach

    He is replaced by Sam Allardyce.

  3. May 2021

    Sam Allardyce leaves as Head Coach

    He is replaced by Valérien Ismaël.

2022-2023

  1. February 2022

    Valérien Ismaël leaves as Head Coach

    He is replaced by Steve Bruce.

  2. October 2022

    Steve Bruce leaves as Head Coach

    He is replaced by Carlos Corberan.

  3. February 2022

    Li Piyue (Chair) and Xu Ke (CEO) leave roles

    Xu Ke remains as a Director. Guochan Lai appointed as Chair, and Ron Gourlay, former Chelsea and Reading CEO, appointed as CEO.

  4. June 2022

    Loan repayment revelations

    The 2020/21 financial accounts show that Lai took out a loan for £4.95 million with 50k interest to help another business. He misses his self-imposed deadline to be repaid by September 2021.

  5. November 2022

    Action for Albion is formed

    Action For Albion is formed in response to the events outlined in this timeline.

  6. December 2022

    £20m loan from MSD Holdings

    MSD Holdings grants WBA a £20m loan to cover "football operations." With interest, WBA will pay back 28m

  7. January 2023

    Lai again fails to repay £5m Loan on second occasion
