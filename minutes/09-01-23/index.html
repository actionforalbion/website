Action For Albion meets The FSA

Meeting notes from 09/01/2023

09/01/2023 Action For Albion meets the FSA

In attendance: Ashley Brown and Jane Hughes of the FSA – (Football Supporters’ Association), Alistair Jones, Paul Faulkner and David Rouse AFA - (Action For Albion), Luke Cox representative of both AFA and FSE – (Football Supporters Europe)

AB explained his role as Head of Governance and Fan Engagement at the FSA. Lots of experience with ‘Crisis Clubs’ and working with other supporters’ groups who were having issues with their club.

JH - EFL Network Manager. Best daily contact for A4A. Also vastly experienced working with other clubs’ supporters’ groups. See’s similarities with the West Brom situation. AB also agreed that they have come across many ‘red flags’ when looking into the current West Brom situation, especially after the information shared by LC.

AB asked for brief run through of the latest situation. PF gave this, describing the timeline from the 2016 purchase of the club, to where we are now, providing details of the ownership structure, as far as we can ascertain. Plus, the loans taken out in the name of Guochuan Lai including one to a non-associated Chinese company.

  1. 1. Those that are fully aware and understand the situation, want change and support AFA.
  2. 2. Those that are aware of the situation but think there is nothing that can be done.
  3. 3. Those that turn up at 3pm, leave at 5pm and are not interested in anything that goes on outside of this.

AB asked if we had a relationship with the senior staff at the club. DR explained that we had reached out to the club, which resulted in a meeting with board directors Ron Gourlay and Ian Skidmore. The meeting went well, and a further meeting is planned for 15 Feb.

AJ introduced himself and reiterated where we had come from and what we were looking to achieve for the good of WBA and the wider football family. Centred around the White Paper and the involvement of the Governments department for Digital Culture Media Sport (DCMS)

AB went on to explain how FSA has helped other clubs and asked us to consider how they can help us and in what specific areas. They have good contacts with experts in various fields including comms, forensic accountancy and supporters who have gone through the same thing. Plus, good connections with sports media, both nationally and locally. FSA’s standing has allowed them to facilitate meetings between the EFL and supporters’ clubs.

AB Stated the DCMS no2 priority is the governance of football. And is confident a lot of things they (FSA) want, will happen. Unfortunately, this is too late for the situation at West Brom. FSA can, however, help by providing support in meetings. Whether that be; WBA, The EFL, Government, etc.

PF summarised what help AFA feel we most need from FSA:

  1. 1. Banking and finance guidance.
  2. 2. Ways of reaching out to the ‘silent majority’ within our fanbase and other WBA supporters’ groups.
  3. 3. Engagement with other club’s fan groups who have been successful.

DR said WBA had always been an inclusive club and that AFA wanted the committee to be of a more diverse nature. AB mentioned a few such groups were members of FSA and would be happy to facilitate/attend introduction meetings. Outside of WBA fan groups, AB recommended dialogue with Blackpool who they can put us in touch with.

AB said that apathy was to be expected from some fans, and this behaviour had been repeated within many other fanbases he had seen, but that negativity towards the workings of AFA should lesson as fans realise, we are peaceful and working on their behalf. All at AFA agreed with this and said we had seen much more positivity and less negativity recently.

AB said they could help with putting AFA in touch with the correct journalists, but they recognised we already had this covered and had seen a lot of exposure already. AFA said this was mainly down to the persistence of AJ and his contacts.

When concluding the call, AB requested AFA send him any documentation we had for his further perusal, and he would visit the new AFA website. It was agreed that FSA/AFA would stay in touch and AB would come back to us with how they could help on the points raised.
