Action For Albion meets Ron Gourlay & Ian Skidmore

Meeting notes from 15/02/2023

15/02/2023 Action For Albion meets Ron gourlay & Ian Skidmore

Location:The Hawthorns, West Bromwich Albion Football Club

Attendees:

  1. 1. Ron Gourlay, Chief Executive Officer, WBA (RG)
  2. 2. Ian Skidmore, Communications Director, WBA (IS)
  3. 3. Alistair Jones, Founder & Committee Member, Action for Albion (AJ)
  4. 4. Brian Chappell, Committee Member (BC)
  5. 5. Paul Chappell, Committee Member (PC)
  6. 6. Paul Faulkner, Committee Member (PF)
  7. 7. Liam Jones, Minute Taker (LJ)

Objectives: A second meeting between representatives of West Bromwich Albion and fan group Action for Albion (A4A) to continue the constructive dialogue that has taken place between the two parties. The meeting centred around five points addressing the concerns of Action for Albion after consultation with fans and members of the committee.

Introduction

During the introduction between both parties, AJ explained that, because of the efforts made by RG & IS to commit to open communication with Action of Albion, the logo for the group had changed to read ‘Support the team, not the owners’, from the original ‘Support the team, not the board’. RG did not know this would happen until he was informed after the change was made public and expressed his gratitude for the attitude of A4A and the peaceful means by which protests have taken place.

Wisdom Smart Loan

AJ raised the issue of the Wisdom Smart loan and whether there had been any direct contact with the company itself by RG or WBA regarding the loan. RG stated that all direct communication regarding the outstanding loan had taken place with Guochuan Lai (GL), the Chairman of the West Bromwich Albion Football Club board.

There was an acknowledgement from IS and RG that they understand why trust has been strained with the fanbase regarding this issue and they also made clear that the message has been received in China regarding fan discontent on this issue. RG again made clear that he fully expects the outstanding sum to be repaid.

AJ further asked RG whether the club had considered taking legal action to receive the money. RG explained that the Wisdom Smart loan is owed to West Bromwich Albion Group, whose sole director is Xu Ke (Ken). It is not within Ron’s remit, as a director of West Bromwich Albion Football Club, to call in the loan owed to Group (a company of which he is not a director). AJ asked if Xu Ke could be present at a future meeting. RG also said he would report back after consulting with Xu Ke. During this discussion, AJ & PF raised the issue of communication with Henry Pu (HP).

Many emails from Shareholders for Albion (S4A) and fans to HP had gone without reply or without an adequate answer. IS pointed out the number of emails that had been received by HP. Further, he made clear that a more efficient communication channel through A4A and S4A, whereby the groups collate questions and put them to the club on behalf of their members, would be a more appropriate mechanism.

Non Offensive Banners

AJ and PF asked how banners in the stadium could be approved for display by the club and whether non-offensive banners were authorised to be displayed. IS made clear that all banners within the stadium require a fire safety certificate, must not block the line of sight of fans, and display non-offensive messages only. The club are open to A4A and others having banners in the stadium provided it is in direct consultation with the club and meets those requirements.

Action for Albion's Next Steps

AJ spoke about the success of A4A thus far in building a clear message and gaining traction on social media, but described this as Phase One for A4A. The next phase includes building on the initial contact made with the Football Supporters’ Association (FSA) and working more closely with other groups to advance the cause. AJ expressed gratitude for the role of the WBA Supporters’ Club (WBASC) but pointed out that WBASC have no website or presence on social media. All A4A representatives expressed a will to work more closely with WBASC to present a united voice for the fanbase. IS and RG acknowledged this but did make clear that RG is in communication with WBASC and other groups across the country.

Personal Meeting with Guochuan Lai

RG has no knowledge of when GL is next due in the UK but will ask for a meeting between the representatives of A4A and GL himself when he is in the country.

Close

The meeting closed with the scheduling of another meeting for 7th March 2023, to take place before the WBA vs Wigan Athletic game at The Hawthorns.
